What is reflective listening and why it is beneficial to your wellness?

  • Reflective (or active) listening is the habit of more careful and skilful listening to what is being said by others.
  • The skill of reflective listening is required more and more in today’s society because of the relentless increase in the ‘pace of life’. As a result, you may
    1. be more frequently rushed
    2. experience a heightened sense of urgency
    3. have more deadlines to be met
    4. have more appointments to be squeezed into apparently ever shorter time scales.
  • You may experience ‘information overload’ as there are many more sources of communication - and they are 24/7 (i.e. 24 hours a day 7 days a week) - such as:
    1. e-mail
    2. mobile phones
    3. text/voice mail
    4. internet including wireless connect from almost anywhere
    5. satellite and cable T.V
  • Direct personal communication has somewhat lessened and our role in the culture of today could be described as one of ‘minimal conversationalists’.
  • You may find yourself
    1. being more impatient when listening to others,
    2. feeling more obliged to complete their statements for them,
    3. not giving sufficient time to try to understand what is really being said,
    4. focussing too often on your own private worlds, looking in as if in a selfinduced trance and thus fail to truly listen to what is being said.

    • This ‘detached’ attitude during conversations prevents you from paying attention to what is being said and may be associated with a higher incidence of physical and psychological ailments.
